Seasonings Guide

Quick pairing tips and safe proportions for everyday cooking.

Garlic Powder

Sweet, pungent, and mellow when cooked

Pairs Well With

  • - All meats and poultry
  • - Roasted vegetables
  • - Potatoes and rice
  • - Pasta sauces

Avoid With

  • - Delicate desserts
  • - Raw preparations
  • - Overpowering amounts

Proportions

Start with 1/2 tsp per lb of meat. Use 1/4 tsp for delicate dishes.

Why I Like It

More convenient than fresh, won't burn like fresh garlic.

Onion Powder

Sweet, savory depth without the bite

Pairs Well With

  • - Beef and pork
  • - Roasted potatoes
  • - Seasoning blends
  • - Soups and stews

Avoid With

  • - Very delicate fish
  • - Sweet desserts
  • - Raw salads

Proportions

Use 1/2 tbs per lb meat. Pairs perfectly with garlic powder 1:1.

Why I Like It

Adds onion flavor without texture or tears while chopping.

Smoked Paprika

Rich, smoky sweetness with vibrant color

Pairs Well With

  • - Chicken and pork
  • - Roasted vegetables
  • - Potatoes and rice
  • - Soups

Avoid With

  • - Delicate white fish
  • - Fresh herbs (can clash)
  • - Too much heat

Proportions

Start with 1/4 tsp per lb. A little goes a long way.

Why I Like It

Adds smoky depth without heat or actual grilling.

Cumin

Warm, earthy, and slightly nutty

Pairs Well With

  • - Ground beef and pork
  • - Beans and lentils
  • - Chili and tacos
  • - Curries

Avoid With

  • - Delicate fish
  • - Sweet dishes
  • - Italian cuisine

Proportions

Use 1/2 tsp per lb meat. Toast whole seeds first for more flavor.

Why I Like It

Essential for authentic Mexican and Middle Eastern flavors.

Oregano

Robust, slightly bitter, and aromatic

Pairs Well With

  • - Tomato-based dishes
  • - Chicken and lamb
  • - Mediterranean vegetables
  • - Pizza and pasta

Avoid With

  • - Mexican foods
  • - Sweet preparations
  • - New to cooking

Proportions

Use 1/2 tsp dried per lb meat. Add near end of cooking.

Why I Like It

Instantly makes anything taste Mediterranean and sophisticated. Adds more formality to Italian Cuisine

Basil

Sweet, peppery, and fresh when dried

Pairs Well With

  • - Tomatoes and mozzarella
  • - Chicken and fish
  • - Pasta and pizza
  • - Mediterranean dishes

Avoid With

  • - Heavy red meats
  • - Spicy dishes
  • - Long cooking times

Proportions

Use 1/4 tsp dried per serving. Add at end to preserve flavor.

Why I Like It

Brightens any dish and pairs perfectly with tomatoes.
